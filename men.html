Jawline Styles

06 shapes
Sharp
Chiseled V-Line

Narrow, clean taper from the jaw angle down to the chin point. The most editorial of the jaw shapes.

Strong
Square Jaw

Wide, even angles on both sides with a flat, block-like chin line. Reads as classically masculine.

Defined
Angular Jaw

A sharp corner where the jaw meets the ear, paired with lean cheeks. Looks best with shorter, textured cuts.

Soft
Soft Rounded Jaw

A gentle curve with less of a hard angle. Balances well with stubble or a tighter beard line.

Balanced
Defined Oval Jaw

Moderate width that curves smoothly into the chin. Versatile and easy to style around.

Tapered
Strong Tapered Jaw

Broad at the angle, narrowing cleanly toward a defined chin point. Pairs well with a fuller beard.

Jawline Exercises

06 moves
Neck
1Neck Curl-Ups

Lie on your back, press your tongue to the roof of your mouth, then lift your head a couple of inches while bringing your chin toward your chest.

3 sets · 10 reps
Chin
2Chin Lifts

Tilt your head back, look toward the ceiling, then stretch your lips upward as if reaching for a kiss. Hold, then release.

3 sets · 10–15 reps
Jaw
3Jaw Clenches

Press your tongue to the roof of your mouth, clench your teeth gently together, and hold before releasing the tension.

3 sets · 10 reps
Posture
4Collarbone Backup

Keep your head level, slowly draw it backward until you feel the muscles on either side of your throat engage, then return to neutral.

3 sets · 30 sec hold
Cheeks
5Vowel Stretches

Slowly and clearly exaggerate each vowel sound — A, E, I, O, U — stretching your mouth fully on every letter.

20 reps daily
Daily
6Chewing Resistance

Chew sugar-free gum (or a firmer mastic gum for added resistance) to gradually build tone in the jaw muscles over time.

15–20 min daily
